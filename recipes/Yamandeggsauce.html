Yam and Egg sause

Description

A beloved West African breakfast staple consisting of hearty, boiled white yam paired with a savory, spicy tomato and onion egg scramble. It’s the ultimate comfort food—simple to prepare, incredibly filling, and packed with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tuber, peeled and sliced into rounds or wedges.
  • 3 to 4 large eggs.
  • 2 medium tomatoes, 1 small onion, and 1 bell pepper (all chopped).
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper (ata rodo), finely minced (optional)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or palm oil.
  • Salt and bouillon powder (like Maggi or Knorr) to taste.

Steps

  1. Peel the yam, slice into rounds, and wash thoroughly. Place in a pot with enough water to cover them, add a pinch of salt, and boil until a fork slides in easily.
  2. While the yam boils, whisk your eggs in a small bowl with a pinch of salt and set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add your chopped onions and fry until translucent, then add the tomatoes and peppers.
  4. Cook the vegetable mixture for about 5–8 minutes until the water has evaporated and the sauce is fragrant.
  5. Pour the whisked eggs over the fried vegetables. Let them sit for a few seconds to set, then gently stir to create large, fluffy chunks.
  6. Drain the water from the yam and serve hot alongside your fresh egg sauce.
